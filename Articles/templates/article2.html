Diabetes Effecting
Low-Income Communities

Diabetes takes the heaviest toll on poor communities, and only adds to the list of problems these communities face. Increasing wealth disparity and middle-class wage stagnation have contributed to the emerging diabetes epidemic.

Lower quality education in low-income communities has caused a low awareness of health and reinforces the loop of poverty. The lack of equal access to healthcare is causing 28% of all diabetes to be unreported.

Did you know...

39% of diabetes patients in Massachusetts faced difficulty with at least one of the following: food, medication underuse, housing instability, and energy instability. This caused poor diabetes control to be seen in 46% of patients.

It is important to realize that this wealth disparity is a integral cause of the spike of diabetes in the past few decades. If we can realize this, we could began to put policies in place that will help these lower income Americans.
