Diabetes is a problem that effects all Americans. Over the last few decades, Diabetes in the US has skyrocketed. It is our job, as Americans, to stop it.

Though diabetes may seem like a personal struggle, there are many overarching environmental effects that have led to this large increase. Many Americans seem to point fingers at many possible causes. However, one seems very critical to understand.

This is the role of income. Income, despite what many Americans may believe, is a very great indicator of diabetes rates. Dennis Raphael, a professor at York University, asserts that income is even a greater indicator of diabetes than someones lifestyle.

Did you know...

According to Professor Raphael's study, you are twice as likely to develop diabetes if you make under $15,000 annually compared to someone who makes over $80,000 annually.

But before we tackle diabetes on a societal level, we must tackle it on a individual one. There are many ways to stay healthy, but the best way to stay healthy is to be informed. Only then we can learn how to stay active and eat right.
