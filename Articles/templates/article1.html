Diagnosing
Diabetes

Because diabetes is so common in the US, are large amount of diabetes in the US goes unreported. At a minimum, no matter how healthy you seem, should schedule a checkup with a doctor once a year. Otherwise, you may over look your symptoms.

Did you know...

According to the CDC, 28% of all diabetes goes unreported. This percentage is greater for lower income communities.

You have prediabetes if...

  • More thirsty than usual
  • Needing to pee a lot often
  • Feeling dehydrated and having a dry mouth and/or skin
  • Blurred vision
  • More hungry and/or tired than usual
    •

    You have type 1 if...

  • You have unexpected weight loss
  • Feeling numbness/pain in your legs/feet
  • Vomiting
    •

    You have type 2 if...

  • Healing process becomes slow
  • Feeling numbness/pain in your legs/feet
  • Yeast infections
    •

    You have diabetes complications if...

  • Lots of itchy skin
  • Greatly reduced healing process
  • Numerous yeast infections
  • Unexpected weight gain
  • Developing acanthosis nigricans which is a skin condition that causes dark, velvety discoloration in the skin
  • Numbness and tingling in feet and hands
  • Reduced vision
    •

    Even if you do not have any of these symptoms, you still may be at risk for diabetes. Type 2 diabetes can has genetic factors which could possibly make you more susceptible to it. The American Diabetes Association, or the ADA, has a online quiz which would tell you your risk for type 2 diabetes.
